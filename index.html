Commit messages

Good commit messages serve at least some important purposes:

  • To speed up the reviewing process;
  • To help developers to find out why a particular change was made to the code or why a specific feature was added;

That said, good commit messages should be:

  • Properly formatted so that they are easy to read in any type of console or application;
  • Self-explanatory so that developers do not need to go out of the log to understand why something happended.
